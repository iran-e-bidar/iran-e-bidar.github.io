Propose Stage

The Propose stage transforms community discussions into concrete constitutional amendment proposals with specific text and implementation details.

Community members create formal proposals that include:

  • Problem Statement: Clear description of the constitutional issue
  • Proposed Text: Exact wording for new or modified articles
  • Rationale: Why this change is necessary and beneficial
  • Impact Analysis: How the change affects other constitutional provisions
  • Implementation Plan: Steps needed to enact the amendment

Proposals must meet constitutional standards:

  • Consistency: Align with existing constitutional principles
  • Clarity: Use precise, unambiguous language
  • Enforceability: Include mechanisms for implementation
  • Rights Protection: Respect fundamental human rights
  • Democratic Values: Support democratic governance principles

ü§ù Collaborative Refinement

Proposals improve through community input:

  • Expert Review: Legal scholars provide technical feedback
  • Public Comments: Citizens share practical concerns
  • Alternative Versions: Multiple approaches to the same issue
  • Language Refinement: Improving clarity and precision
  • Compromise Solutions: Balancing different viewpoints

üìä Proposal Types

Different types of constitutional proposals:

  • Rights Amendments: Adding or clarifying fundamental rights
  • Structural Changes: Modifying government institutions
  • Process Improvements: Better democratic procedures
  • Clarifications: Resolving ambiguous constitutional language
  • Modernization: Updating outdated provisions

üöÄ Moving to the Next Stage

A proposal advances to the Review stage when:

  • The proposal text is complete and well-drafted
  • Initial community feedback has been incorporated
  • The proposal has sufficient community interest
  • All required documentation is provided
